# South Forsyth Information Architecture

This document outlines a scalable information architecture for SouthForsyth.org as a premium local guide for South Forsyth, Georgia. The structure is designed to support long-term growth into a large topical publishing system with strong internal linking, search friendliness, and AI-ready content organization.

## Core principle
Build around topical authority rather than isolated pages. Every section should connect to adjacent content through clear hubs, related links, breadcrumb navigation, cards, and category-based collections.

## Internal linking strategy
- Use hub pages to connect related sections.
- Link from every major section to the homepage, the nearest parent category, and at least three nearby topical pages.
- Add related-content modules to every section.
- Use breadcrumbs on interior pages to reinforce structure.
- Include category-based “Explore more” links in sidebars and footers.

## SEO strategy
- Target one primary keyword cluster per page.
- Use clean, descriptive URLs and consistent title patterns.
- Include structured data where relevant.
- Create topical clusters rather than isolated, one-off articles.
- Support discovery with category pages, guides, and evergreen content.

## AI search strategy
- Use clear headings and concise summaries.
- Provide FAQ-style sections where appropriate.
- Make information easy to interpret and quote.
- Ensure entities such as places, organizations, and events are consistently labeled.
- Keep metadata and page structure predictable for machine readability.

## Recommended content types
- Landing pages for each major section
- Directory entries for businesses and services
- Guide articles for weekend planning and moving
- Event entries for upcoming programming
- News articles for civic and community updates
- Seasonal and holiday roundups

## Growth plan
- Start with the highest-intent pages first: Home, Weekend Guide, Things To Do, Events, Restaurants, Schools, Neighborhoods, New Resident Guide, and Business Directory.
- Add supporting pages next: Parks, Trails, Playgrounds, Healthcare, Government, Weather, Traffic, and Local News.
- Expand into deeper content once the core structure is live, including historical content, volunteer information, and community organization profiles.
